Swiss Cities

Medieval charm, modern culture, and mountain backdrops — Switzerland's cities are unforgettable

Zurich

Zurich is Switzerland's largest and most cosmopolitan city. It consistently ranks among the world's most liveable cities and is famed for its thriving banking sector, design culture, and nightlife. The charming Altstadt (old town) lines both banks of the Limmat River, packed with guild houses, boutique shops, and excellent restaurants.

  • Grossmünster cathedral — twin towers with panoramic views
  • Bahnhofstrasse — one of the world's most exclusive shopping streets
  • Kunsthaus Zurich — Switzerland's largest art museum
  • Lake Zurich — perfect for swimming and boat trips
  • Langstrasse — Zurich's buzzing nightlife district

Zurich at a Glance

Language: German

Population: ~440,000

Known for: Finance, art, nightlife, Lake Zurich

Airport: Zurich International (ZRH)

Best time: June–August, December

Geneva

Geneva is the most international city in Switzerland. Home to the United Nations European HQ, the Red Cross, and dozens of other global organisations, it has a cosmopolitan, sophisticated atmosphere. The Jet d'Eau fountain shooting 140 metres into the air over the lake is the city's iconic landmark.

  • Jet d'Eau — the famous water fountain on the lake
  • Old Town & St Peter's Cathedral
  • Palais des Nations — UN headquarters tours available
  • The CERN particle physics laboratory (guided tours)
  • Watch and jewellery boutiques in the Rue du Rhône

Geneva at a Glance

Language: French

Population: ~200,000 city / ~600,000 metro

Known for: Diplomacy, watches, chocolate, Lake Geneva

Airport: Geneva International (GVA)

Best time: April–October

Bern

Bern, the federal capital, is one of the best-preserved medieval cities in Europe and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The city is famous for its 6 kilometres of covered arcaded walkways called Lauben, which protect pedestrians from rain and snow, and its beloved bear pit.

  • The Zytglogge — a stunning medieval clock tower
  • Bear Park — home to the city's symbolic bears
  • Rosengarten — rose garden with panoramic city views
  • Paul Klee Centre — world-class art museum
  • Bern Historical Museum

Bern at a Glance

Language: German

Population: ~140,000

Known for: Politics, medieval arcades, bears

Status: Federal Capital

Best time: May–September

Lucerne

Often described as the most beautiful city in Switzerland, Lucerne sits on the edge of a stunning lake surrounded by mountains. Its covered wooden Chapel Bridge — dating from 1333 — and picturesque old town make it a photographer's dream. The city is also a gateway to famous peaks like Mount Pilatus and Rigi.

Basel

Situated where Germany, France, and Switzerland meet, Basel is the cultural capital of Switzerland. It hosts Art Basel, the world's premier contemporary art fair, and has over 40 museums. Its Rhine river promenade and vibrant market squares make it perfect for leisurely exploration.
